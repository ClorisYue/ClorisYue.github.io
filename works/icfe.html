In Context for Educators is a new Gale product specifically for educators which meets their needs in finding curriculum standard based and personalized materials with existing curriculum standards, topics and curriculum plans, curating and using the materials by clipping, sharing, saving and collaboration.

2018 Summer UX Intern @ Gale, Cengage Learning | Team of 2 with one Senior UX Designer | Duration: June, 2018 - Aug, 2018 | Tools: Axure, Sketch
Status: Designed concepts for design evaluations. The product is planned to launch around September 2019.

Design Challenge

While preparing teaching materials and classes, K-12 educators have several needs:

  • Find replacement and supplemental materials align to curriculum standards.
    Teachers usually get a set of state curriculum standards, an overall teaching goals or plans from the school. They have to find according materials and carefully schedule the syllabus and each class.
  • Collaborate and curate the materials to meet their needs.
    When teachers find materials, they may need to clip parts of the materials, edit the content, annotate the edited materials, and even collaborate with others, like librarians.
  • Use the materials.
    Teachers need to share the original or edited material to individuals or a group, and allow discussion of the materials.

Currently there is no product which fully covers all these needs, so the preparing process requires a lot of effort to find, curate and use the materials manually. As one of the biggest content provider in the US, it's an opportunity for Gale, Cengage Learning to provide an ecosystem with rich content where educators can prepare all their curriculum standards aligned materials.

Design Overview

1. Find - Setting Active Curriculum Standards

  • Set an active curriculum standard while on-boarding which will be applied to the future search. Usually an educator uses only one set of standard according to the grade, subject and the school requirement.
  • Change the active curriculum standard. Educator may change the standard after a term.

2. Find - Upload An Existing Curriculum Document

  • Upload the document. Educator usually has unit plans which links subjects areas with standards in hand.
  • Analyze the document. The system automatically analyzes the document and extracts the teaching topics and standards, and does a batch search.
  • Browse the materials aligned to related topics and standards.

3. Curate - Create Collection and Collaborate

  • Add the selected materials to a collection.
  • Manage the materials, collaborators and collections.
  • Clip the material.

4. Use materials

This need is not highlighted in the concept, because the first two features have been realized in current Gale products which can be easily integrated into the design. And the add-on feature is out of the current product scope.

  • Share the original or curated materials to Learning Management Systems.
  • Download or print appropriate format of materials.
  • Add-on feature: "decorate" the materials like embedding multimedia into a reading, linking reference readings to terminologies, and creating discussion on the teaching materials in a class.

Prioritize user needs and define product scope

After analyzing research results, we categorized and listed all user needs and created 12 personae for major needs. In order to prioritize user needs, in the meanwhile, define the scope of product and make sure it is sell-able and technical feasible, then we conducted a persona mapping session with VP, PM, tech lead, sales, marketing, and specialists to get one primary persona, two secondary personae and three tertiary personae.

Roles and Goals Matrix

Find overlapping and special needs

After persona mapping, I created a roles and goals matrix with hierarchic goals and the 6 selected personae. For each persona I marked all the possible goals. After finishing this matrix, some overlapping goals and special goals for some personae can be obvious. And the weight of each goal was different because of the importance of each persona, which should also be taken into consideration while ordering the goals. Through the matrix, we got the priority of goals, which are important to our product.

  • Find curriculum standard aligned materials
  • Create collections within the product
  • Curate materials within a group I create
  • Clip parts of the resources
  • Annotate materials for the people I share with
  • Download the resources in specific format
  • Share materials to students

Roles and goals matrix. Dark red marks the uppermost goals, with light red for goals of the next level, and most specific goals with no background.

User Scenario

Understanding the context and all possible user paths

For each selected persona, we analyzed the user scenario, to be more specific, what materials they'll have in hand, how the materials related to curriculum, the anticipated user path and other objectives.

All user Scenario

Using our primary persona, Henry, as an example, to achieve the same goal - finding replacement materials, there might be several different scenarios. For one case, when Henry has a school district scope and sequence, or a unit plan, he may need to find all materials related to the topics or standards in these documents and develop the course outline. If Henry is interested in a topic, he can search the topic to find materials and see how they tied to standards and integrate the topic into teaching. Similarly, if he has a specific standard or teaching objective to meet, he needs to find the materials align to it.

Workflow

Synthesize user paths for various design concepts

Based on the scenarios and important goals we got, we found there are several possible user paths. We together designed several variations and integrated into two concepts. I'll focus on the concept I was responsible for.

The main workflow, shown in the following image, is based on our primary persona Henry who has unit plan and curriculum standard related materials on hand. For Henry to plan the class outline it's tedious to search all replacement materials for each teaching topic and check the standard alignment to ensure that he's using the correct materials. So it'll be helpful for him to upload the existing document and got all related materials at one time. And it's based on the fact that most educators will only use one standard at one time, so the system will automatically apply the active standard to the search. But this is not a common way to do filter in the education products, future user evaluations are needed. So, another workflow will be set the standard filter before every search if needed and always search by topics, which developed by another designer.

Reflection

It's a little pity that I cannot follow the project from ideation to launch, but I really appreciate the opportunity to design for real-industry problems which are quite challenging but exciting. Real-industry problems have many limitations which I may not think about before. For example, we usually will not release sudden changes during midterm to avoid training costs for our customers, and all the copyright issue behind sharing in our products.

What's more, I realized the difficulty of designing a new product. We spent two months analyzing research results, creating our personae and defining the scope of product. And involved people from other groups in several important meetings to get everyone on the same page, decide the overall project time-line, technical feasibility, and so on. And I learned to create personae based on a rich search result with great variety and map personae taking suggestions from different perspectives.

If I have more time...

  • Do user evaluations to test on our various concepts and iterate.
  • Curriculum standards and materials alignment is a great technical challenge for our product, which also leads to possible design decisions to compensate the inaccuracy of algorithm.
  • Although Cengage has a rich database of primary resources, articles, books, and so on, we do not have enough supplemental materials, such as lesson plans, class activities, which need specialists to create for now. Maybe in we can make use of user generate content, and let users to assign materials to standards in the future. But how to assess that the alignment is reliable can be another question.

In Context for Educators is a new Gale product specifically for K-12 educators, which meets their needs in finding, curating and using curriculum standard based and personalized materials with existing curriculum standards, topics and curriculum plans.

2018 Summer UX Intern @ Gale, Cengage Learning | Mentor: Thomas (Senior UX Designer) | Duration: June, 2018 - Aug, 2018 | Tools: Axure, Sketch

Design Challenge

While preparing teaching materials and classes, K-12 educators have several needs, and the three major needs are:

  • Find replacement and supplemental materials align to curriculum standards.
    Teachers usually get a set of state curriculum standards, an overall teaching goals or plans from the school. They have to find according materials and carefully schedule the syllabus and each class.
  • Collaborate and curate the materials to meet their needs.
    When teachers find materials, they may need to clip parts of the materials, edit the content, annotate the edited materials, and even collaborate with others, like librarians.
  • Use the materials.
    Teachers need to share the original or edited material to individuals or a group, and allow discussion of the materials.

Currently there is no product which fully covers all these needs, so the preparing process requires a lot of manual effort. As one of the biggest content provider in the US, it's an opportunity for Gale, Cengage Learning to provide an ecosystem with rich content where educators can prepare all their curriculum standards aligned materials.

Design Overview

1. Find - Setting Active Curriculum Standards

  • Set an active curriculum standard while on-boarding.
  • Change the active curriculum standard.

2. Find - Upload An Existing Curriculum Document

  • Upload the document.
  • Analyze the document.
  • Browse the materials aligned to related topics and standards.

3. Curate - Create Collection and Collaborate

  • Add the selected materials to a collection.
  • Manage the materials, collaborators and collections.
  • Clip the material.

4. Use materials

This need is not highlighted in the concept, because the first two features have been realized in current Gale products which can be easily integrated into the design. And the add-on feature is out of the current product scope.

  • Share the original or curated materials to Learning Management Systems.
  • Download or print appropriate format of materials.
  • Add-on feature: "decorate" the materials like embedding multimedia into a reading, linking reference readings to terminologies, and creating discussion on the teaching materials in a class.

Roles and Goals Matrix

Overlapping needs like finding curriculum standard aligned materials, and special needs like clipping parts of the resources are found.

After persona mapping, I created a roles and goals matrix with hierarchic goals and the 6 selected personae. The weight of each goal was different because of the importance of each persona. Overlapping goals and special goals for some personae can be obvious in the matrix. Through the matrix, I got the priority of goals, which are important to our product.

  • Find curriculum standard aligned materials
  • Create collections within the product
  • Curate materials within a group I create
  • Clip parts of the resources
  • Annotate materials for the people I share with
  • Download the resources in specific format
  • Share materials to students

Reflection

Consider the constraints of real-industry problems

It's a little pity that I cannot follow the project from ideation to launch, but I really appreciate the opportunity to design for real-industry problems which are quite challenging but exciting. There are some constraints for this project which I may not be able to meet in a course project. For example, we usually will not release sudden changes during midterm to avoid training costs for our customers, and all the copyright issue behind the sharing feature in our products.

Difficulty of scoping a new product

We spent almost two months analyzing research results, creating our personae and defining the scope of product. And involved people from other groups in several important meetings to get everyone on the same page, decide the overall project time-line, technical feasibility, and so on. And I learned to create personae based on rich research results and map personae taking suggestions from different perspectives.

If I have more time...

  • Do user evaluations to test on our various concepts and iterate.
  • Curriculum standards and materials alignment is a great technical challenge for our product, which also leads to possible design decisions to compensate the inaccuracy of algorithm.
  • Although Cengage has a rich database of primary resources, articles, books, and so on, we do not have enough supplemental materials, such as lesson plans, class activities, which need specialists to create for now. Maybe in we can make use of user generate content, and let users to assign materials to standards in the future. But how to assess that the alignment is reliable can be another question.

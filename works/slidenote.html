00. The Background

SlideNote Introduction Video

It’s common to see students, researchers and businessmen taking photos of lecture or meeting slides, or writing down contents of slides in a hurry. Under these circumstances, there are problems like:

  • Listeners cannot get the slides
  • It‘s too slow to write down all important contents
  • Too much photos will take up lots of storage
  • It’s hard to manage and review the notes and slides
    •

The idea of SlideNote came from our daily life. Based on class, meeting, training and other similar scenarios, it focuses on users’ need to get the contents of slides and make notes more efficiently.

03.User Interview

Our users are divided into four groups, considering two key behaviors: taking photos of slides and taking notes by hand. Since the groups of people who take photos of slides will give us more hints on design, we planned interviews.

We interviewed three college students and two graduate students and three people who work in companies. These people always attend classes, meetings, conferences in which slides play an important role.

Summarizing these interviews we get some design inspirations:

  1. Since more people take photos with their mobile phones, we can design an IPhone version which focuses more on photo taking, recognition and quick notation. The startup homepage of IPhone version is the camera page which allows users to recored slides swiftly. And they can add notation directly on the photos and insert photos into notes. After lecture, they can recognize the text in the photo again.
  2. However, the IPad version focuses not only on text recognition but note editing with lots of inputs. And provide both single-column and double-column mode to meet individuals' habitat of taking notes.
  3. Considering different users’ ways of organizing and classifying their notes. SlideNote allows users to organize their notes into at most three layers, and their photos into two layers.
  4. To improve the efficiency and effectiveness of taking notes, note contents are associated with according slides; users can add notation quickly by hand with highlighter, pen or pencil.

06. Reflection

Fig. From left to right Qing, Kaiwei, Yahan, Weizhou, Yue, Jiehui

The team was composed of 4 graduate students major in Interaction Design in school of Communication and Design, SYSU. I attended Dr. Shaoke Zhang's seminar because of interest and met the team leader. I joined the team to practice my design skills, in the meanwhile, as a undergraduate student from School of Data and Computer Science I provided technical support and recruited engineering students to help with the demo. We were so excited to won the first place of 2016 China Mobile Application Innovation Tournament (about 400 teams in total) and got the opportunity to consider whether we wanted to start our own business or not.
